Advocacy & Foundation Networks

These international non-profits provide emotional support networks, family-to-family connection programs, localized equipment closets, and educational conferences.

Spastic Paraplegia Foundation (SPF)

The primary organization in North America dedicated exclusively to finding cures and managing treatments for Hereditary Spastic Paraplegias (HSPs). SPF offers extensive research grants, local support circles, and medical webinars.

Visit SP-Foundation.org ↗

National Organization for Rare Disorders (NORD)

A global leader representing over 30 million Americans affected by rare conditions. NORD hosts rare-disease databases, financial assistance programs, and coordinates advocacy campaigns.

Visit RareDiseases.org ↗

EURORDIS — Rare Diseases Europe

A patient-driven alliance representing more than 1,000 rare-disease organizations across Europe, connecting national support bodies with central clinical guidelines.

Visit Eurordis.org ↗

Scientific & Genetic Registers

Academic resources that host peer-reviewed descriptions, clinical phenotypic checklists, diagnostic algorithms, and active research registers.

GeneReviews® — SPG11 Overview

A high-authority NIH resource detailing homozygous spastizin-related genetic conditions. Provides clinicians and researchers with updated phenotype descriptions, mutation spectrums, and genetic counseling parameters.

Read GeneReviews SPG11 ↗

Orphanet Rare Disease Directory

The primary European portal for rare diseases and orphan drugs, offering detailed classifications of autosomal recessive spastic paraplegias, including expert consensus documents.

Explore Orphanet Database ↗

ClinicalTrials.gov (NIH Register)

A database of privately and publicly funded clinical studies conducted around the world. Search "SPG11" to discover active natural history studies, biomarker discovery protocols, and investigative therapeutic trials.

Search active studies ↗

Physical Therapy & Equipment Resource Networks

Because managing progressive motor stiffness requires high-quality structural aids, caregivers are strongly encouraged to seek equipment loan closets, posture positioning experts, and customized seating professionals. Check with local chapters of the United Cerebral Palsy (UCP) or general **HSP Foundations** regarding active equipment donation systems.
